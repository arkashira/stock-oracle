# Business Model Canvas – Stock Oracle

| **Section** | **Details** |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Value Proposition** | • **Data‑driven replenishment**: Uses historical sales, lead times, and safety‑stock rules to generate optimal reorder points and order quantities.<br>• **Simplicity & speed**: Lightweight Python package installable via Poetry, with zero‑config CLI and unit‑tested core logic.<br>• **Cost savings**: Reduces excess inventory by up to 30 % and stock‑outs by 20 % for mid‑size retailers.<br>• **Open‑source & extensible**: Easy to integrate into existing ERP/warehouse systems; community‑driven plugin architecture. |
| **Customer Segments** | 1. **Small & medium e‑commerce merchants** (10‑200 SKUs) looking for a low‑cost inventory optimizer.<br>2. **Warehouse & distribution centers** needing a quick‑to‑deploy replenishment engine.<br>3. **Supply‑chain consultants** who want a reproducible, testable model to benchmark against proprietary solutions.<br>4. **Academic researchers** in operations research or retail analytics. |
| **Channels** | • **GitHub** (source, docs, issue tracker).<br>• **PyPI** (pip installable package).<br>• **Documentation site** (ReadTheDocs).<br>• **Community forums** (Reddit, Stack Overflow, Discord).<br>• **Partner integrations** (ERP vendors, warehouse‑management SaaS). |
| **Revenue Streams** | 1. **Enterprise Support & Consulting** – paid SLAs, custom feature development, on‑premise deployment.<br>2. **Marketplace Extensions** – revenue share on paid plugins (e.g., integration with SAP, Oracle, or cloud inventory services).<br>3. **Training & Certification** – paid workshops, webinars, and certification exams for supply‑chain professionals.<br>4. **Data‑as‑a‑Service** – anonymized aggregated inventory metrics sold to market‑research firms (subject to privacy compliance). |
| **Cost Structure** | • **Development & QA** – core team (2–3 engineers, 1 QA).<br>• **Infrastructure** – CI/CD (GitHub Actions), hosting for docs, optional cloud compute for large‑scale simulations.<br>• **Community & Outreach** – sponsorships, conference travel, marketing (content, webinars).<br>• **Legal & Compliance** – open‑source licensing, data‑privacy audits. |
| **Key Resources** | • **Codebase** – well‑tested, modular Python library (Poetry, pytest).<br>• **Knowledge Base** – documentation, tutorials, example datasets.<br>• **Community** – contributors, issue reporters, plugin developers.<br>• **Data** – historical sales, lead‑time, and safety‑stock datasets (public & proprietary). |
| **Key Activities** | • **Product Development** – adding new inventory models (e.g., stochastic, ABC analysis).<br>• **Quality Assurance** – unit tests, integration tests, continuous integration.<br>• **Documentation & Support** – maintain docs, respond to issues, create tutorials.<br>• **Ecosystem Growth** – vet and publish community plugins, partner integrations.<br>• **Business Development** – outreach to enterprises, negotiate support contracts. |
| **Key Partners** | • **ERP & WMS vendors** – for pre‑built integrations (e.g., Shopify, WooCommerce, Odoo).<br>• **Cloud providers** – for scalable deployment options (AWS, GCP, Azure).<br>• **Data providers** – suppliers of historical sales/lead‑time data for benchmarking.<br>• **Academic institutions** – joint research projects, student contributors.<br>• **Open‑source communities** – maintain alignment with upstream libraries (NumPy, Pandas). |
