# Product Requirements Document
## Stock Oracle
### Overview

The Stock Oracle project aims to develop a simple yet effective inventory replenishment planning system. This document outlines the product requirements, target users, goals, key features, success metrics, and scope for the Stock Oracle project.

### Problem Statement

Inventory management is a critical aspect of business operations, particularly in industries with high-volume sales or perishable products. Inefficient inventory replenishment planning can lead to stockouts, overstocking, or dead stock, resulting in significant financial losses and decreased customer satisfaction. The current manual processes for inventory replenishment planning are time-consuming, prone to errors, and often rely on intuition rather than data-driven insights.

### Target Users

The primary target users of the Stock Oracle system are:

* Inventory managers
* Supply chain managers
* Procurement teams
* Business owners

These users will utilize the system to optimize inventory levels, reduce stockouts, and minimize excess inventory.

### Goals

The primary goals of the Stock Oracle project are:

1. **Automate inventory replenishment planning**: Develop a system that can analyze historical sales data, seasonality, and other factors to generate accurate replenishment plans.
2. **Improve inventory turnover**: Optimize inventory levels to reduce stockouts, overstocking, and dead stock, resulting in improved inventory turnover and reduced waste.
3. **Enhance user experience**: Provide an intuitive and user-friendly interface for inventory managers to input data, view replenishment plans, and receive alerts and notifications.

### Key Features (Prioritized)

1. **Historical sales data analysis**: Integrate with existing sales data systems to analyze historical sales trends, seasonality, and other factors that impact inventory demand.
2. **Automated replenishment planning**: Develop algorithms to generate replenishment plans based on analyzed data, taking into account lead times, supplier constraints, and other relevant factors.
3. **Inventory level optimization**: Calculate optimal inventory levels based on replenishment plans, considering factors such as storage capacity, handling costs, and inventory holding costs.
4. **Alerts and notifications**: Implement a notification system to alert users of potential stockouts, overstocking, or other critical inventory events.
5. **User interface**: Design an intuitive and user-friendly interface for users to input data, view replenishment plans, and receive alerts and notifications.

### Success Metrics

The success of the Stock Oracle project will be measured by the following metrics:

1. **Inventory turnover ratio**: Monitor the improvement in inventory turnover ratio, indicating reduced stockouts and overstocking.
2. **User adoption**: Track the number of users adopting the system and the frequency of use.
3. **User satisfaction**: Conduct regular surveys to measure user satisfaction with the system's performance, usability, and overall value.
4. **Return on investment (ROI)**: Calculate the ROI of the system by comparing the costs of implementation and maintenance with the benefits of improved inventory management.

### Scope

The scope of the Stock Oracle project includes:

* Developing a simple inventory replenishment planning system
* Integrating with existing sales data systems
* Designing an intuitive user interface
* Implementing automated replenishment planning and inventory level optimization algorithms
* Developing a notification system for alerts and notifications

The following items are out of scope:

* Developing a full-fledged enterprise resource planning (ERP) system
* Integrating with supplier systems for automated ordering
* Implementing advanced analytics or machine learning models for demand forecasting

By focusing on the key features and success metrics outlined in this document, the Stock Oracle project aims to deliver a valuable and effective inventory replenishment planning system that meets the needs of its target users.
